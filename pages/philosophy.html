My Philosophy

Why no trackers and ads?

Today, it has become almost impossible to use a simple online tool without being bombarded by ads or tracked by dozens of scripts. Why?

I believe that for utilities as simple as these, trackers and ads serve no real purpose for the user. On the contrary, they slow down the experience and invade privacy.

The worst part is that this practice often only enriches the advertising industry itself, and very little the independent developer. It’s a "lose-lose" system for humans.

Simplicity and Open Source

ToolsBoXMachine (TBXM) was born from a desire to get back to basics: tools that do one thing, and do it well, without fluff.

I firmly believe in Open Source. Code must be transparent, auditable, and shareable. Most tools run locally in your browser. No tracking, no storage — just pure functionality.

Centralization and Community

We've all been there: searching for a specific tool and ending up with 40 tabs open just to find one that actually works, without asking for an account or payment.

My goal is to centralize as many essential utilities as possible on a single platform. No need to look elsewhere—it's all here, in one place, with the same smooth interface.

And because it’s an Open Source project, it’s yours too. If you're missing a tool or have a great idea, you can suggest it directly on GitHub via Issues. That’s the community spirit.

My Vision

Creating useful things shouldn't require compromising your values. TBXM will always remain pure, fast, and respectful of your time and privacy.

— Drayko
